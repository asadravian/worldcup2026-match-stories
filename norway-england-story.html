Part one

How it happened

Six moments decided this match. The scroll bar is your match clock.

0' Kick-off

Hot, muggy, hostile

A July afternoon in South Florida, 64,000 inside Hard Rock Stadium. Norway arrive having never been this far. England arrive having survived Mexico in the round of 32, rescued that night by Bellingham.

36' Goal · Norway

Schjelderup stuns England

Andreas Schjelderup gives Norway the lead against the run of England's early pressure. The red wall of Norwegian fans erupts. Haaland's pre-match mind games suddenly look justified.

45+2' Goal · England

Bellingham answers before the break

On the stroke of half time, Jude Bellingham strikes. Just as he did against Mexico, England's number 10 shows up exactly when the game demands it. 1–1 at the interval, and the momentum flips.

2H VAR · Goal disallowed

Norway's winner that wasn't

From a corner, Torbjørn Heggem bundles the ball home and Norway think they lead again. Video review rules it out: Haaland pushed an England player to the ground just before the delivery. Regulation ends 1–1.

90+3' Goal · England

Bellingham. Again.

Deep into added time, a loose ball breaks England's way and Bellingham buries the winner, his second of the night. Norway protest furiously, and not without reason. More on that below.

120' Full time · AET

England hold on through extra time

Thirty more minutes in the heat, a Norwegian yellow card at 117', and England see it out. Norway's greatest ever World Cup run ends. England march on to a semi-final against Argentina.

Part four

Two calls Norway will replay forever

The disallowed goal

Haaland's push

Heggem's second-half finish from a corner was ruled out on video review after Haaland shoved an England player to the ground moments before the kick was taken. From potential 2–1 lead to nothing in ninety seconds.

The sky cam question

Did the wire change the game?

Replays suggested the Norwegian keeper's clearance before Bellingham's winner clipped the overhead sky cam wire, changing the ball's flight before it dropped to England. FIFA said the ball's sensor data showed no evidence its trajectory was affected. Norway's bench disagreed. Loudly.
